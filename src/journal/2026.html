06/11/2026 - 5:27PM:
Last night was the last straw for me, I officially ended the awl (Arcade.Wrapper-Linux) cycle, its development had been slow for a thousand reasons.
The main reasons are that I developed a hatred for the original project (TeknoParrot), for its creators and for my own project, they have a DLL with an "anti-debug" syscall that acts by "obfuscating" the mapping (which was often done desperately in the dark).
And another reason was because I was going crazy alone, I mapped more than 50 games in about 4 months completely, I didn't have support or help from a single good soul in the community, I'm not going to screw myself over alone in this while the assholes from the original project are neglecting it and still getting paid for it.

I'm tired.

My focus has returned entirely to my studies in C/ASM, and to the free development of Vault, my lightweight mini-launcher CLI for games and Wine programs, AppImages, and Tarballs for Unix-like systems.

06/01/2026 - 2:49PM:
Today, I started a new project, but I got so tired and hyper-focused that I even forgot to talk about it in my Journal.
It's nothing extremely complex, but it's extremely necessary.

It's an extremely small and lightweight "mini launcher CLI" for games and programs, being extremely useful for non-Steam games and old Wine games, but completely usable in modern games, as long as the user knows how to prepare their own prefix after Vault creates it.
